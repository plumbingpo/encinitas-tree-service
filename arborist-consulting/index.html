The Benefits of Professional Arborist Consulting

Arborist consulting requires specific credentials, training, and professional standards that general tree service companies may not possess. ISA certification demonstrates that an arborist has passed rigorous testing on tree biology, diagnosis, management, and safety, and maintains their knowledge through continuing education. For consulting work â€” especially reports that will be used for legal, insurance, or municipal purposes â€” the credentials of the consulting arborist directly affect the credibility and acceptance of the report.

Professional consulting arborists also carry professional liability insurance (errors and omissions coverage) that protects you if their professional advice is later found to be incorrect. This level of professional accountability is essential for high-stakes consultations involving property disputes, construction projects, and insurance claims.

How to Prevent Arborist Consulting Problems

A little prevention goes a long way in avoiding expensive tree care emergencies. Follow these tips from our certified arborists:

  • Engage a consulting arborist early in construction projects â€” before plans are finalized
  • Get a tree assessment before purchasing property with significant trees
  • Document your trees with photos and professional assessments for insurance purposes
  • Develop a long-term tree management plan with professional guidance
  • Consult an arborist before making major decisions about tree removal or pruning
  • Keep copies of all arborist reports for your property records
  • If trees are damaged by a third party, document immediately and consult an arborist for appraisal
